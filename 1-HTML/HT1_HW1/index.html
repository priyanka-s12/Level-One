Biography

Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi, commonly known as Mahatma Gandhi, was a prominent leader in the Indian independence movement. He was born on October 2, 1869, in Porbandar, India. Gandhi is best known for his philosophy of nonviolent resistance and civil disobedience, which he calleld "Satyagraha."

Philosophy

Ahimsa (Nonviolence)

Mahatma Gandhi believed in the power of nonviolence as a means to bring about social and political change. He practiced and preached the principle of "Ahimsa," which advocated nonviolent resistance even in the face of oppression.

Satyagraha (Truth Force)

gandhi's concept of "Satyagraha" emphasized the pursuit of truth and justice through nonviolent means. It became a cornerstone of his struggle for India's independence.

Legacy

Mahatma Gandhi's legacy is profound and continues to inspire people worldwide. His nonviolent approach to change has influenced civil rights movements, leaders, and activists globally. He played a pivotal role in India's fight for independence from British rule and is often referred to as the "Father of the Nation" in India.
